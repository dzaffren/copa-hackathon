PART A

OVERVIEW

1 Introduction

1.1 Financial institutions continue to expand their use of technology to deliver
financial services, and this exposure draft revisits the Bank's expectations on
technology risk management in light of that continued expansion. Financial
institutions must-
(a) invest in the required expertise and risk controls to prevent operational
disruptions given the complexity of Information Technology (IT) systems;
(b) achieve a high level of security to combat fast-changing digital crimes
and preserve public confidence;
(c) maintain robust oversight to prevent transmission of risks arising from
increased third-party interlinkages; and
(d) build operational resilience against sophisticated and malicious cyber
threats.

1.2 This exposure draft revises and consolidates the Bank's expectations on
technology risk management previously issued, and reflects the Bank's
observations from ongoing supervisory engagement with the industry on the
adoption of cloud services and emerging technology.

2 Applicability

2.1 This policy document is applicable to financial institutions as set out in the
list of applicability above.

2.2 Where a provision of this policy document only applies to a subset of
financial institutions, this is stated expressly in that provision.

3 Legal provisions

3.1 This policy document is issued pursuant to-
(a) sections 47(1), 143(1) and 266 of the Financial Services Act 2013 (FSA);
(b) sections 57(1), 155(1) and 277 of the Islamic Financial Services Act 2013
(IFSA); and
(c) sections 41(1), 116(1) and 126 of the Development Financial Institutions
Act 2002 (DFIA).

4 Effective date

4.1 This exposure draft, once finalised, is proposed to come into effect on a date
to be announced, save for any transitional arrangements to be set out
separately.

5 Interpretation

5.1 The terms and expressions used in this policy document shall have the same
meaning as defined in the FSA, IFSA or DFIA, as the case may be, unless
otherwise defined in this policy document.

5.2 For the purposes of this policy document, "critical system" refers to a system,
the failure of which would cause significant disruption to the operations of a
financial institution or impact its customers.

6 Related legal instruments and policy documents

6.1 This policy document must be read together with other relevant legal
instruments and policy documents that the Bank may issue from time to
time, including but not limited to the policy documents on Outsourcing,
Business Continuity Management, and Operational Resilience.

7 Policy documents and circulars superseded

7.1 Upon issuance, this policy document is intended to supersede the previous
version of the policy document on Risk Management in Technology.

PART B

POLICY REQUIREMENTS

8 Governance

8.1 The board and senior management of a financial institution are responsible
for the sound management of technology risk, and must ensure that an
effective technology risk management framework is in place.

8.2 The board must approve the financial institution's technology risk appetite
and ensure it remains consistent with the overall risk appetite of the
financial institution.

8.3 Senior management must designate an appropriate officer or board-level
committee responsible for reviewing and confirming the financial
institution's readiness prior to significant technology adoptions, including
the adoption of public cloud services for critical systems.

9 Technology Risk Management

9.1 A financial institution must establish a technology risk management
framework commensurate with its size, complexity of operations, and risk
profile.

9.2 The technology risk management framework must be reviewed periodically
to remain relevant to emerging technology risks.

10 Technology Operations Management

10.1 A financial institution must implement sound technology operations
management practices, including in relation to change management,
capacity management, and system resilience.

Cloud Services

10.50 A financial institution must fully understand the inherent risk of adopting
cloud services. In this regard, a financial institution shall conduct a
comprehensive risk assessment prior to cloud adoption which considers the
inherent architecture of cloud services that leverages on the sharing of
resources and services across multiple tenants over the Internet. The
assessment must specifically address risks associated with the following:
(a) sophistication of the deployment model;
(b) migration of existing systems to cloud infrastructure;
(c) location of cloud infrastructure including potential geo-political risks and
legal risks that may impede compliance with any legal or regulatory
requirements;
(d) multi-tenancy or data co-mingling;
(e) vendor lock-in and application portability or interoperability;
(f) ability to customise security configurations of the cloud infrastructure to
ensure a high level of data and technology system protection;
(g) exposure to cyber-attacks via cloud service providers;
(h) termination of a cloud service provider including the ability to secure the
financial institution's data following the termination;
(i) demarcation of responsibilities, limitations and liability of the cloud
service provider; and
(j) ability to meet regulatory requirements and international standards on
data protection and cross-border data transmission.

11 Cybersecurity Management

11.1 A financial institution must establish a cybersecurity framework that is
commensurate with its risk profile and the threat landscape it faces.

11.2 The cybersecurity framework must be reviewed and updated periodically to
address emerging cyber threats.

12 Digital Services

12.1 A financial institution offering digital services must implement adequate
controls to safeguard the confidentiality, integrity and availability of
customer information processed through such services.

13 Technology Audits

13.1 A financial institution must conduct periodic technology audits to assess
the effectiveness of its technology risk management framework.

14 External Party Assurance

14.1 A financial institution must obtain independent assurance on the
technology risk management practices of its critical external service
providers, where appropriate.

15 Security Awareness and Education

15.1 A financial institution must implement an ongoing security awareness and
education programme for its employees.

16 Notification for Technology-Related Applications

16.1 A financial institution must notify the Bank of any technology-related
application or enhancement to its digital services, in accordance with the
requirements set out in this section.

16.2 The notification must include a description of the technology-related
application and an assessment of the associated risks.

16.6 A financial institution shall provide relevant documents for the Bank's review
when required by the Bank.

16.7 A financial institution may offer digital services or implement any
enhancement to the digital services immediately upon submission of the
notification under paragraph 16.1 and compliance with the requirements in
paragraphs 16.2 to 16.6.

17 Cloud services

17.1 A financial institution shall notify the Bank within 14 days of the first-time adoption of a public cloud service for a critical system, having first:
(a) completed the risk assessment under paragraph 10.50;
(b) a senior management and board readiness confirmation; and
(c) an independent third-party pre-implementation review.

17.2 A financial institution shall notify the Bank of any subsequent adoption of a
public cloud service for a critical system, by submitting the notification
together with the necessary updates to all the information required under
paragraph 17.1. Such subsequent notification relies on and draws directly
from the consultation under paragraph 17.1, and the financial institution
must confirm that the risk assessment, readiness confirmation and
independent pre-implementation review carried out for that prior
consultation under paragraph 17.1 remain valid and have been updated, as
necessary, to reflect the subsequent adoption. Where the prior consultation
under paragraph 17.1 has not been completed, or its outcomes are no
longer current, a financial institution may not rely on paragraph 17.2 and
must instead complete the requirements of paragraph 17.1 afresh.

17.3 For the avoidance of doubt, notification to the Bank under paragraph 17.2 is
not required for any enhancement to existing cloud adoption that does not
materially alter the prior assessments and representations made by a
financial institution to the Bank under paragraph 17.1.

17.4 The Bank may at its discretion direct a financial institution to comply with
paragraph 17.1 or 17.2, including for a non-critical system or when the
pre-requisites in paragraphs 17.1 or 17.2 have not been met. A financial
institution must comply with such a directive promptly and to the
satisfaction of the Bank.

17.5 A financial institution must ensure the roadmap for adoption of cloud
services for critical systems and non-critical systems is included in the
annual outsourcing plan submitted to the Bank in adherence with the
requirements in the policy document on Outsourcing. The risk assessment
outlined in paragraph 10.50 must also be documented and made available
for the Bank's review as and when requested by the Bank.

18 Assessment and Gap Analysis

18.1 A financial institution must perform a gap analysis of existing practices in
managing technology risk against the requirements in this policy document
and highlight key implementation gaps. The financial institution must
develop an action plan with a clear timeline and key milestones to address
the gaps identified.

18.2 The self-assessment, gap analysis and action plan in paragraph 18.1 must
be submitted to the relevant supervisory department of the Bank, as the
case may be.
