- **Section:** Section 129(2) — transfer of personal data to places outside Malaysia

## Verbatim excerpt (`PDPA 129`, current law)

> A data controller may transfer any personal data of a data subject to any place
> outside Malaysia if—
> (a) there is in that place in force any law which is substantially similar to this
> Act; or
> (b) that place ensures an adequate level of protection in relation to the processing
> of personal data which is at least equivalent to the level of protection afforded by
> this Act.

**Why it matters (RMiT 17.1):** a cloud region outside Malaysia engages the PDPA's
cross-border transfer test — the destination must have substantially similar law or
adequate protection — so the 17.1 notification should still capture data residency once
the requirement to consult the Bank is removed.

## Provenance — how this reconstructs to current law

The **pre-2024** text (which the spec previously quoted) was:

> 129. (1) A data user shall not transfer any personal data of a data subject to a place
>      outside Malaysia unless to such place as specified by the Minister, upon the
>      recommendation of the Commissioner, by notification published in the Gazette.

Act A1727 §12 amends §129 by: **(a)** deleting subsection (1) [the Minister/Gazette
whitelist]; **(b)(i)** substituting, in subsection (2), "For the purposes of subsection
(1), the Minister may specify" with "A data controller may transfer any personal data of
a data subject to"; **(b)(ii)** deleting ", or that serves the same purposes as this Act"
in paragraph (a); plus the global "data user" → "data controller" rename. Applying that
to the verbatim 2010 subsection (2) yields the current excerpt above — both the original
text and the amendment instructions were extracted verbatim from the real gazette PDFs.
