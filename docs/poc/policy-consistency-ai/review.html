    📡 Reference Radar

    what external sources say about this topic

    For the clause you are drafting — 17.1, public-cloud adoption — Rulebook Radar surfaces the sources that lead the draft: peer regulators, national acts, international bodies, the regulatory handbook, and technology trends. Each entry cites its source.

    Peer regulator · Singapore

    Technology Risk Management Guidelines (MAS, 2021)

    Cited source · sample excerpt

    "…the FI does not require the Authority's prior approval to adopt public cloud services; the FI remains responsible for carrying out due diligence and managing the technology risks on an ongoing basis…"

    Why it matters: a peer central bank already governs cloud adoption through risk management and outsourcing controls, not pre-approval — the benchmark for 17.1's shift from consultation to notification.

    National act · Malaysia

    Personal Data Protection Act 2010 — s.129 (transfer outside Malaysia)

    Cited source · sample excerpt

    "A data user shall not transfer any personal data of a data subject to a place outside Malaysia unless to such place as specified by the Minister…"

    Why it matters: a cloud region outside Malaysia engages the PDPA's transfer limits — the 17.1 notification should capture data residency so this check is not lost when consultation goes.

    International body

    Principles for Operational Resilience (Basel Committee, 2021)

    Cited source · sample excerpt

    "Banks should manage their dependencies on relationships, including those of, but not limited to, third parties or intragroup entities, for the delivery of critical operations."

    Why it matters: the international baseline keeps responsibility for third-party (incl. cloud) dependencies with the bank regardless of the approval model — 17.1 must preserve that expectation.
